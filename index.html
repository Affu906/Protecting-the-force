Introduction / Purpose

Build cyber awareness for police personnel by highlighting threats, safe digital behaviour, and incident-response fundamentals — for both official and personal device usage.

Objectives

  • Educate about phishing, malware and social engineering.
  • Promote MFA, strong passwords and secure communication.
  • Encourage timely reporting and basic incident response steps.
  • Support awareness campaigns with reusable learning material.

Scope of Awareness

  • Target audience: police staff + general public.
  • Focus: digital hygiene, safe devices, data protection.
  • Content: guides, case studies, survey, media, forum.
  • Outcome: reduced cyber risk through awareness.

Research Findings

Abstract/summary, charts & graphs, key insights, and downloadable full research paper.

Summary

The research highlights common cyber risks affecting police and the community such as phishing, weak authentication, device compromise, and data leakage. It emphasizes training, secure practices, and fast reporting to reduce operational impact.

Key Insights
  • Most common entry point: phishing and impersonation.
  • Weak passwords + no MFA increases account takeover risk.
  • Updates and device hygiene reduce malware incidents.
  • Clear reporting steps reduce damage and response time.

Awareness Tips

Do’s & Don’ts, step-by-step guides, safety practices, and real-life stories/case studies.

Do’s

  • Enable MFA on official accounts.
  • Verify sender identity before clicking links.
  • Update OS/apps and use approved security tools.
  • Use official channels for sensitive communication.

Don’ts

  • Don’t share OTP/passwords or case details on chat apps.
  • Don’t install apps from unknown sources.
  • Don’t use public Wi-Fi for confidential tasks.
  • Don’t ignore security alerts/suspicious logins.

Safety Practices

  • Separate personal vs official device usage.
  • Lock screen + auto timeout on devices.
  • Use backups where policy allows.
  • Report incidents immediately.

Step-by-Step Guides

  1. Check sender domain carefully (typos are common).
  2. Hover on links to preview real URL.
  3. Look for urgency/threat language.
  4. Verify via official contact method.
  5. Report to IT/cyber cell; do not forward to others.
  1. Update OS/apps; remove unused apps.
  2. Enable PIN/biometric + auto-lock.
  3. Disable unnecessary permissions.
  4. Enable MFA for email and official apps.
  5. Turn off Bluetooth/NFC when not needed.
  1. Disconnect from network (if possible).
  2. Inform supervisor/IT/cyber cell immediately.
  3. Change passwords from a clean device + revoke sessions.
  4. Preserve evidence (screenshots/logs) as per policy.
  5. Follow incident response procedures.
