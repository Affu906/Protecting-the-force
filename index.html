Protecting the Force
“Cyber aware police, cyber safe citizens”

Do’s

Use strong, unique passwords; enable multi-factor authentication; verify sender details; lock devices when not in use; update software regularly.

Don’ts

Don’t share OTPs, credentials or sensitive case info on social media or personal messaging apps; don’t plug unknown USB drives into police systems.

Report & Respond

Whenever suspicious activity is detected, record evidence (screenshots, logs) and report quickly to the cyber cell or national cyber crime portal.


Safety Practices (AI Enabled)

AI systems can flag suspicious logins and unusual access patterns. Police staff must review these alerts and take action quickly.

Real-Life Case Study (India)

Scenario: In a metro city, fraudsters created a fake UPI helpdesk account and called citizens pretending to be from the bank. One police officer also received such a call on his official number.

Instead of sharing any OTP, the officer captured the caller ID, took screenshots of the messages and reported it immediately to the cyber crime branch. Using digital trails and CCTV from the ATM where money was withdrawn, the gang was tracked and arrested.

Lesson: Never share OTP or PIN on calls, even if the person claims to be from a bank or police. Report suspicious calls to the official cyber helpline.
