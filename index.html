3. Awareness Tips

Practical guidance for police personnel and citizens – do’s & don’ts, step-wise approach and real-life cybercrime stories.

DO’S & DON’TS

Safe Digital Habits

Do: Use strong passwords, enable 2-factor authentication, verify links before clicking, report suspicious messages to cyber cell.

Don’t: Share OTP / PIN, install unknown apps, respond to prize / KYC messages or transfer money without verification.

STEP-BY-STEP GUIDES

Awareness Campaign Flow

Identify → Research → Plan → Create → Launch → Engage → Feedback → Improve. Simple instructions explaining how to start a cyber awareness drive inside a police station or community.

SAFETY PRACTICES

Safety Practices for Police

Use official email only, secure case files and evidence drives, lock screen when leaving desk, never plug unknown USB devices and follow cyber hygiene SOPs issued by the department.

REAL-LIFE STORIES / CASE STUDIES

UPI Fraud Case Study (Example)

A fraudster created a fake “customer care” number. The victim called, shared OTP and lost money from account.

Lesson: Banks / police never ask for OTP. Victims must immediately call helpline 1930 and file a complaint on the National Cyber Crime Portal.

7. Community Forum / Discussion

Space where participants can share experiences or ask questions during the awareness program (demo only).

SHARE EXPERIENCES / ASK QUESTIONS
Sample Post: “I received a call saying my bank KYC is pending and I must click a link. Is this genuine?”
Reply: This is likely a phishing attempt. Never click unknown links. Visit your bank branch or official website directly.
GROUND RULES

Safe & Respectful Discussion

• Do not share real personal data (account numbers, phone, address).
• Use case IDs instead of victim names.
• Respect privacy and follow IT Act & cyber-crime guidelines.
• Escalate serious cases to official channels instead of only forum.
